Architecture

The CIS is organized in layers. L1 holds the REA primitives — the economic ontology that makes cooperative accounting possible. L2 holds the audit trail. Planned waves will add patronage, treasury, coordination, and membership tooling on top of this substrate.

What is live

L1 Substrate LIVE
Five core tables seeded and queryable: agents, agreements, agreement_sections, resources, role_assignments. REA ontology in place.
Bylaws v2 DRAFT
187 sections across 18 articles plus preamble, loaded with stable addressing. Draft status — not yet ratified. Two known issues tracked in metadata.
RLS Policies LIVE
Row-level security on all tables. Bylaws are publicly readable. Members, directors, and admins have role-gated access. Cite-as-you-enforce pattern with bylaw citations in policy names.
Audit Trail LIVE
Triggers on all L1 tables write to audit.events automatically. Append-only. Members can read their own events; directors see all.
Bootstrap Agents LIVE
Three agents seeded: RegenHub LCA (the cooperative itself), Nou (collective intelligence AI), Dianoia (execution intelligence AI).
Member Auth PLANNED
Supabase Auth not yet provisioned on this project. W1 will wire login, map auth.uid() to agents.id, and enable member-gated views.
Patronage Engine PLANNED
Capital accounts, contribution primitives, and year-end allocation logic. W2. Validated engine exists (184 tests); needs schema migration.
Hub Operations PLANNED
Workspace bookings, co-working membership, space revenue tracking. W3+. Intranet rebuild will surface this data.

What the CIS is not

The CIS is not a workflow tool, a chat surface, or a task manager. It holds the economic facts of the cooperative — what exists, what was agreed, what happened. Applications are built on top of it, but the CIS itself is the ground truth.

The Workshop coordination system (co-op.us) will be retired. The intranet will be rebuilt directly from this CIS project rather than migrated from the legacy system.
