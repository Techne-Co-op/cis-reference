Who can see and do what — and how roles are granted
Overview Schema Policies Roles Bylaws Migrations

The CIS defines 15 roles in the role_assignments table. Roles are time-bounded grants — each has a start date, an optional expiry, and a grantor. The RLS helper has_role() checks active assignments at query time.

Assignment mechanism: Role grants are written to role_assignments by directors or admins. Authentication is via Supabase Auth (W1 — not yet provisioned). Until W1, the CIS holds role data but cannot enforce it against real login sessions. Bootstrap roles assigned to AI agents are active.

Member Journey

Guest Public materials only. Reads bylaws and benefit report.
Applicant Under board review. Reads bylaws and membership agreement. No member records.
Patron Member Admitted. $100 share. One vote. Member directory, all agreements, own capital account.
Director Board elected. Full read/write. Governs role grants and agreement creation.

Cooperative Roles

Roles are derived from the bylaws. Access is not a separate permissions layer — rights and obligations are defined in the bylaws and enforced at the data layer.

Guest
Public-facing materials only: benefit report, principles, aggregate figures. Bylaws are readable without authentication.
bylaws:v2:art-15 — §XV
Applicant
Own application and signed agreements. No access to member records. Can read bylaws and membership agreement before signing.
bylaws:v2:art-1.sec-4 — §1.4
Patron Member
Own records always. Aggregate figures and membership list on request. Meeting materials for meetings they attend. Eligible for patronage allocation. One vote. $100 share.
bylaws:v2:art-1.sec-2, §1.7, §2.9, §18.1
Investor Member
Own records always. Class-specific voting where required. No patronage allocation. No workspace access. Terms pending Pote Law Firm (Class Four).
bylaws:v2:art-1.sec-3, §2.6.2
Suspended Member
Read own records only. No voting, no participation in management. Status reviewed at next board meeting.
bylaws:v2:art-1.sec-10 — §1.10.1
Former Member
Historical access to own records, including in-progress redemption schedule. Capital account paid out on board schedule (up to 5 years).
bylaws:v2:art-1.sec-11
Director
Member access plus board materials, board minutes, committee reports. Full write access. Governs role grants and agreement creation. Elected by Patron Members.
bylaws:v2:art-3 — §III
Officer
Role-specific authority: Treasurer over financial records, Secretary over minutes and registers, President over contracts. Appointed by the board.
bylaws:v2:art-4 — §IV
Committee Member
Director access plus full committee working materials. Appointed by the board to serve on standing or special committees.
bylaws:v2:art-3.sec-18 — §3.18
Administrator
Operational access for onboarding, support, and data stewardship. Does not create substantive decisions. Full database access. Typically a technical steward or AI agent.
Policy role — not a membership class
Agent (Nou)
Scoped impersonation: inherits permissions of the invoking user, never more. All actions logged. Nou is the collective intelligence agent; Dianoia is execution intelligence.
